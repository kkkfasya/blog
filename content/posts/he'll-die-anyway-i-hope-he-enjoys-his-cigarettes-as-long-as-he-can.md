&emsp;My dad who's been a heavy smoker since his late teenage year is still alive as per i wrote this. He's aging, he's growing old, he looks stressed—he's such a hardworking man, providing his best to his family, and he also look sad—the kind of sad old people have that you can't really explain until you hit certain age. My mom always belittles him about his smoking habit—it's for good reason—and i do agree with her until recently.  
&emsp;I in no way downplay the dangerous effect of smoking, as i myself do smoke—though not as heavy as my dad does, yet still i feel a tremendous impact towards my physical—be it performance or appearance. 
With recent pictures of him that i saw, i have to emotionally prepare for his death and i think i'm capable of doing so, financially however it will take such a toll on my life, but i'm prepared alas. Given his not so long life duration, i very much hope he dies after i'm able to give him a Seiko watch, i know which one that he'll really love.  
&emsp;Now this begs the question whether or not he should stop smoking and get a healthier lifestyle or to continue his current ones until he dies. After a quick teary-midnight-pondering, the latter is much more appealing, why? Not that i don't love to see him improving his life, but what for? He's not going to live that long—as much as i want to, he will not, and also undoing all the fuckery he had done to his body will cause him utmost misery—his body isn't accustomed to healthy lifelstyle, again, for what? Just so he can live a year longer? No years prolonged matter if it's full of agony, the moment matters more than the duration.
All i hope for is him to not die in ill like my grandpa did—laying in bed, unable to utter words clearly, unable to play chess. Stroke is the enemy of life. The best possibility there is is for him to die of heart attack, either during his sleep or while he's smoking his favorite cigarette—preferably ones that are bought by me. My dad will enjoy his last cigarettes, my dad will sleep in peace, and he will die in peace. He should not worry about his skinny asshole child, nor his fat greedy child, he will die in peace, besides his lovely wife.
