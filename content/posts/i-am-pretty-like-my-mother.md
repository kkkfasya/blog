>"Every women is my mom, except my mom. She is a woman"   

&emsp;I said to my friend, Bagas, late at night, this monday, 23rd of March 2026. "The fuck is that supposed to even mean, man" He said. To no surprise he was as confused as i am. "What do i know, forget about it, i said things i myself don't understand sometimes" I replied. Morning comes, i woke up late—as always—then in an instant, i think of what i had just said last night. I cried, a few tears touched my bedsheet, then conclusion comes.

>"Every women is my mom, except my mom. She is a woman"   

&emsp;That every women, in some way—directly or indirectly—bear a resemblance to my mother, and that my mother, failed to fulfil her role. Very unfortunate realization i had. With that, i found the answer as to why i can't—for the hardest i tried—love a woman to its fullest extent. That, to love her up until her last strain of her hair, i have to love the other strain that reminds me of my mother.
